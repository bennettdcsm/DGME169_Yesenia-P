About Me

Embedded systems engineer focused on hands-on hardware, robotics, and tools that make research and everyday life easier.

Who I Am

My name is Yesenia Puga, and I’m an embedded systems engineer who enjoys building things that live in the real world, robots, smart sensors, and tools that support research. I studied Computer Engineering at the University of California, Santa Cruz, where I focused on hardware, C programming, and low-level systems.

Since then, I’ve worked on projects that connect microcontrollers, sensors, and software, including an autonomous hospital sanitation robot, an ESP32 soil moisture monitor with an iOS app, and a Python-based annotation tool for neuroscience images called Fine-Tune.

Design Focus & Motivation

I’m most motivated by projects that help people in a direct way. That includes supporting hospital staff with robotics, giving researchers better tools to review brain data, and helping students feel confident when they touch a microcontroller for the first time. I like breaking big problems into smaller pieces, testing as I go, and leaving behind something that’s reliable and documented.

Skills & Strengths

  • Embedded Systems (ESP32, Arduino)
  • C / C++ / Python / MicroPython
  • I2C, SPI, UART, BLE communication
  • Robotics & state machine design
  • Image processing & ML tools
  • SwiftUI iOS integration
  • Leading workshops & mentoring students
